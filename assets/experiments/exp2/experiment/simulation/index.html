Fault Scenarios

Inject physical wear and mechanical faults to observe degradation in control stability.
Simulates severe seat wear. Injects 10% base flow at 0% opening and induces steady-state hunting.
Procedure:
1. Drag signal to 0%.
2. Observe that flow never reaches 0 L/min.
3. Notice the 3D valve stem and flow numbers hunting violently.

Explanation: The physical seal inside the valve is broken. High pressure fluid forces its way through the gap, rattling the metal stem and preventing complete shutoff.
Introduces static friction. Creates a 10% dead-band requiring extra controller signal to break free.
Procedure:
1. Slowly drag the signal slider.
2. Observe that the "Actual Stem Pos" refuses to move initially.

Explanation: The mechanical stem is rusted/jammed. Your controller sends the signal, but the stem is physically stuck until enough air pressure builds up to violently snap it free.
Reduces pilot air to 2 bar. Valve loses holding force and slowly drifts closed under high pressure drops.
Procedure:
1. Drag signal to 100%.
2. Watch the "Actual Stem Pos" bleed out and drift closed against the high pressure drop.

Explanation: The pneumatic actuator has lost its 5-bar air supply. It no longer has the mechanical strength to push against the high-pressure water, so it gets slowly forced closed by the fluid.
