Task 1 — Steady-State Angle Measurement

Steps the transmitter through 0°, 30°, 60° … 330°, waits for the receiver to synchronise (≤ 3 s), and records the settled angle and error ε = |shaft − receiver| at each point.

Expected: steady-state error ≤ ±0.3°; sync time ≈ 1 s; uniform accuracy across all quadrants.

Task 2 — Dynamic Tracking

Rotates the shaft 0° → 360° over 10 s (36°/s) and samples the receiver every 100 ms, then reports the measured lag and peak tracking error.

Expected: lag 50–100 ms; tracking error < 1° at low speed; the receiver may hunt (oscillate) due to damping.

Task 3 — Receiver Load Effect

Applies increasing friction-load levels to the receiver rotor and repeats a steady-state step (0° → 180°) at each level to find the maximum load the receiver can hold sync against.
